Catalan (White)

The catalan opening starts:
1. d4 d5 2. c4 e6 3. g2 Nf6 4. Nf3
It aims to exert long term presure on the queenside. This is accomplished by grabbing space with the c and d pawns along with fianchettoing the powerful catalan bishop on g2. This opening has two main variations, the open catalan and the closed catalan. The open catalan is reached if black takes the c pawn with his d pawn while the closed catalan is reached when black refuses to take the c pawn. The open catalan has many deep variations that generally result in white either reclaiming the gambited c pawn or receiving adequate compensation. The closed catalan (and the catalan in general) is a very positional opening. Focus is put on improving piece placement and maximizing the pressure applied on the queenside. It is a solid opening with Lichess's masters database reporting a 28% winrate for white, 53% draw, and 19% win for black.

French Defense (Black)

The french defense opening starts with the moves:
1. e4 e6 2. d4 d5
This opening is seen as very solid for black and black often equalizes very early in the game. Emphasis is placed on either queenside expansion or an f pawn break depending on the position. There are many variations, but some of the most common are the advance variation, winawer variation, and exchange variation. The advance variation is when white plays 3. e5. It is met with pressure on the d pawn and general queenside expansion by black. The winawer variation is when white plays 3. Nc3. It is very sharp with tactics for both sides. The exchange variation begins with 3. exd5. It is the most solid of the three and often ends in a draw.

Queens Gambit (white)

The queen's gambit begins with the moves
1.d4 d5 2. c4
It is one of the oldest and most studied openings in chess and often transposes into other commonly played openings. White attempts to weaken blacks central control with a flank pawn (c pawn). Black can either accept the gambit or decline it. Accepting the gambit is generally seen as bad for black because it allows white to develop pieces more easily and have an active position. Declining the gambit leads to an equal position with black having a variety of options to continue.
